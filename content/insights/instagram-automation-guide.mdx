Instagram automation is tricky. Do it wrong, you get shadowbanned. Do it right, you save 10+ hours per week.

Here's what works in 2024.

## What Instagram Allows

Instagram's official API lets you:
- Schedule posts and stories
- Respond to comments and DMs
- Analyze performance metrics
- Manage multiple accounts

What it DOESN'T allow:
- Auto-following/unfollowing
- Auto-liking
- Auto-commenting
- Engagement pods

### 1. Content Scheduling

### 2. Comment Management

**What to automate**:
- Comment monitoring
- Spam detection
- Response suggestions

**What NOT to automate**: Actual responses. Instagram can detect bot-like behavior.

### 3. Story Automation

**What to automate**:
- Story templates
- Highlight organization
- Story scheduling

## What Gets You Banned

Avoid these:
- Third-party apps that ask for your password
- Auto-follow/unfollow services
- Engagement pods
- Buying followers
- Mass DM campaigns

Instagram's AI is smart. It knows when you're using bots.

## Best Practices

**Do**:
- Use Instagram's official API
- Mix automated and manual content
- Respond to comments personally
- Post consistently (3-7x per week)
- Use relevant hashtags (10-15 per post)

**Don't**:
- Post the same content across all platforms
- Use banned hashtags
- Ignore your audience
- Post only promotional content
- Use automation for engagement

## Measuring Success

Track these metrics:
- Engagement rate (likes + comments / followers)
- Reach (how many people see your posts)
- Profile visits
