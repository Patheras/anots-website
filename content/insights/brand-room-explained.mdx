Every marketing automation tool claims to "understand your brand." Most of them mean "paste your brand guidelines into a text box."

**Brand Room is different.**

## The Problem with Traditional Brand Management

You've seen this before:

1. Sign up for a marketing tool
2. Manually enter your brand colors (hex codes, one by one)
3. Upload your logo (hope it's the right format)
4. Copy-paste your brand voice guidelines
5. Pray the AI actually follows them

Then your brand evolves. New colors. Updated fonts. Refreshed messaging. And you have to update everything manually. Again.

## How Brand Room Works

Brand Room doesn't ask you to input your brand. **It excavates it.**

### Multi-Source Intelligence Extraction

Give Brand Room any combination of:

- **Website URL**: Scrapes colors, fonts, logo, layout patterns
- **Documents**: PDFs, brand guidelines, style guides
- **Social Media**: Instagram, LinkedIn, Twitter profiles

Brand Room analyzes everything and extracts:

- **Visual Identity**: Logos (all variants), color palettes (primary, secondary, accent), typography (font families, weights, sizes)
- **Brand Voice**: Tone patterns, vocabulary preferences, messaging style
- **Content Patterns**: Post structures, hashtag usage, emoji patterns

### The Magic Import Flow

1. **Paste your website URL** (or upload documents, or connect social media)
2. **Wait 30-60 seconds** while Brand Room excavates
3. **Review the extracted brand profile** (colors, fonts, voice, everything)
4. **Approve or adjust** (you're always in control)
5. **Done** - ANOTS now knows your brand

No manual data entry. No hex code hunting. No copy-pasting guidelines.

## Why This Matters

When Qubik (our creative AI) generates a social post, it doesn't just "use your brand colors." It:

- Matches your exact color palette (primary, secondary, accent)
- Uses your font families and weights
- Writes in your brand voice (formal, casual, playful, technical)
- Follows your content patterns (hashtags, emojis, structure)

When Themis (our review AI) checks the content, it validates:

- Color usage matches brand guidelines
- Tone is consistent with your voice
- Visual hierarchy follows your patterns

## Real Example

A design agency gave Brand Room their website. In 45 seconds, it extracted:

- 3 logo variants (full, icon, wordmark)
- 8-color palette (primary purple, secondary grays, accent colors)
- 2 font families (Inter for UI, Playfair for headings)
- Brand voice: "Professional but approachable, technical but not jargon-heavy"

Then they ran 50 ANOTs. Every single output matched their brand. No manual tweaking.
